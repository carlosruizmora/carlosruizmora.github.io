Presentation

Carlos Ruiz, Assistant Professor at Universidad Carlos III de Madrid (UC3M), Department of Statistics. He received his B.S. in Industrial Engineering (2007) from the Universidad de Castilla-La Mancha and his PhD in Electrical Engineering from the Universidad de Castilla-La Mancha (2012) under the supervision of Professor Antonio J. Conejo. He has been a postdoctoral researcher at Supélec and École Centrale (Paris, France) and at Universidad Carlos III de Madrid (2013-2014). He has published over 20 papers in JCR journals and presented his work in several international conferences such as INFORMS or ISMP.

Research Interest

My research interest includes almost everything that may lie in the intersection of Operations Research, Analytics and Energy Systems. The main mathematical and OR tools that I am using or developing include:

  • Single and multi-objective programming problems. Linear, nonlinear and mixed-integer.
  • Robust optimization.
  • Stochastic programming.
  • Inverse optimization.
  • Multi-stage optimization problems. Bilevel, trilevel, etc.
  • Duality and complementarity modeling.
  • Agent based models.
  • Inverse optimization.
  • ARIMA, Regression, SVM and clustering models.

The main energy systems' topics that are being addressed by using these mathematical tools are:

  • Modeling and characterization of oligopolistic behaviors in the electricity market.
  • Generation capacity expansion planning.
  • Transmission expansion planning of the electricity network.
  • Pricing non-convexities in the electricity market.
  • Supply chain coordination within the electricity industry.
  • Integration and operation of Smart grid technologies.
  • Reliability of transmission and distribution systems.
  • Demand load, renewable and price forecasting in energy systems.
